Strategic Insights for Investors

Actionable network intelligence for FinTech deal sourcing in India. All data verified.

01

IIT System Dominates ‚Äî But It's Not Just IIT Delhi

The IIT system produced 39+ founders across Delhi (16), Bombay (12), Kharagpur (6), Kanpur (3), and Roorkee (2). IIT Delhi leads, but IIT Bombay is close behind and has the highest density of same-company co-founders (4 from Khatabook alone).

02

The Flipkart Mafia Is the #1 Company Network

10 founders came from Flipkart ‚Äî more than any other company. Notably, entire founding teams emerged: all 4 PhonePe co-founders and all 4 Groww co-founders previously worked at Flipkart. Monitor Flipkart/PhonePe/Groww alumni for the next wave.

03

ICICI Bank Is the Banking Talent Factory

9 founders are ICICI alumni ‚Äî the highest of any financial institution. OneCard alone has 4 of 5 co-founders from ICICI. The consumer banking and credit card divisions appear to be the specific training ground for FinTech entrepreneurship.

04

Small Payments Companies Breed Unicorn Founders

Citrus Payment (4 founders ‚Üí Pine Labs, Jupiter, OneCard, Open) shows that small, early payments startups can be surprisingly effective incubators. Monitor current payment startups for tomorrow's founders.

05

IIT + IIM Combo Is the Power Profile

The most successful founder profile combines IIT undergraduate + IIM MBA: Yashish Dahiya (IIT-D + IIM-A + INSEAD), Ashneer Grover (IIT-D + IIM-A), Sarbvir Singh (IIT-D + IIM-A), Lokvir Kapoor (IIT-K + IIM-B).

06

DCE/DTU: Small but Mighty

Only 2 founders in this dataset, but one of them is Vijay Shekhar Sharma (Paytm) ‚Äî India's biggest FinTech success. DCE/DTU punches above its weight relative to representation.

07

Bengaluru = Where You Build, Not Where You Studied

43% of companies are HQ'd in Bengaluru, but IIT Delhi (not Bengaluru) dominates education. The Delhi‚ÜíBengaluru pipeline is the strongest migration pattern: study in the north, build in the south.

08

Batchmate Co-Founding Is a Recurring Pattern

Khatabook (4 IIT Bombay batchmates), Razorpay (2 IIT Roorkee batchmates), Money View (2 IIT Delhi alumni), CoinDCX (2 IIT Bombay alumni). College classmate networks are a strong predictor of co-founding teams.

09

Chennai Is Undermonitored

Zoho ‚Üí Chargebee (3 of 4 co-founders), M2P, Yubi, Vivriti Capital. Chennai has a distinct tech-to-FinTech pipeline that receives less investor attention than Bengaluru or Mumbai.

10

Networks to Monitor for Deal Sourcing

Priority watch list: (1) IIT Delhi/Bombay alumni groups, (2) Flipkart/PhonePe/Groww alumni leaving for startups, (3) ICICI Bank consumer banking departures, (4) Citrus/PayU payments alumni, (5) Zoho alumni in Chennai, (6) Google India teams, (7) McKinsey India consultants transitioning to startups.
